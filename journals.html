November 22, 2025 Industry Exposure

TaRSIER 117: Learning About Emergency Response Systems

Today, I had the privilege of visiting and learning about the Telephone and Radio System Integrated Emergency Response (TaRSIER 117) in Bohol. As an IT student, seeing a real-world critical infrastructure system in action was eye-opening and showed me how technology literally saves lives.

What impressed me most was the integration of multiple communication channels - landline, mobile, radio, and VoIP - working seamlessly together. The system operators demonstrated how they coordinate emergency responses across the entire province, handling everything from medical emergencies to disaster management.

Key insights and learnings:

  • Understanding redundancy: Multiple communication channels ensure the system never fails
  • Real-time coordination: How dispatchers manage simultaneous emergencies efficiently
  • System reliability: The importance of uptime in mission-critical systems
  • Community impact: Technology's role in public safety and disaster preparedness
  • Career perspective: IT isn't just about apps and websites - it's about building systems that matter

This experience made me realize the profound responsibility IT professionals have when building critical systems. It's not just about code - it's about the lives depending on that code working flawlessly.

November 21, 2025 EdTech Experience

Discovering CodeChum: A Student's Programming Journey

As a student, discovering CodeChum has been a game-changer for my programming studies. This Philippine-based platform, created by fellow Cebuano programmers, understands exactly what Filipino students need to succeed in tech.

What makes CodeChum different from other learning platforms is the instant feedback. Every time I submit code, I immediately know if it works or not - no waiting for professors to check manually. The automated grading system has helped me learn faster because I can iterate and improve my solutions right away.

Student insights and learnings:

  • Self-paced learning: I can practice coding anytime, even at midnight when inspiration strikes
  • Practical problems: The challenges mirror real programming scenarios, not just theory
  • Multiple languages: Learning C, Python, and Java all in one platform saves time
  • Filipino context: Built by Filipinos for Filipinos - they understand our education system
  • Skill building: My debugging skills improved dramatically with immediate feedback

CodeChum taught me that programming isn't just about memorizing syntax - it's about problem-solving. As a student, having this tool has made me more confident in my coding abilities and better prepared for real-world development work.

November 21, 2025 Industry Visit

Industry Exposure at Rivan School of Technology

Visiting Rivan IT (Rivan School of Technology) opened my eyes to what professional IT training looks like. As a student used to classroom learning, seeing their training labs filled with real Cisco routers, switches, and enterprise equipment was impressive - this is what the real world uses!

The instructors shared their experiences working abroad and how proper training and certifications opened doors for them globally. They emphasized that Filipino IT professionals are respected worldwide, but we need the right skills and credentials to compete.

Key insights from the visit:

  • Hands-on matters: Touching real equipment beats reading slides any day
  • Certifications count: CCNA and CompTIA are globally recognized credentials
  • Global opportunities: Proper training can lead to jobs in Singapore, Dubai, or the US
  • Practical labs: Their cloud labs mean students can practice anytime, anywhere
  • Career paths: Networking, cybersecurity, and cloud computing are high-demand fields

This visit made me realize that college education is just the foundation - specialized training like what Rivan IT offers is what makes you industry-ready. I'm now seriously considering pursuing certifications alongside my degree to be more competitive.

November 21, 2025 Career Guidance

Career Planning: Learning from Worldtech Information Solutions

Visiting Worldtech Information Solutions in Davao gave me clarity on my career path. As a third-year IT student, I was overwhelmed by choices - should I focus on programming, networking, security, or cloud? The career counselors helped me understand the certification landscape and how to build a strategic career path.

They explained how certifications like ITIL, CompTIA, and Cisco aren't just pieces of paper - they're proof of standardized knowledge that employers worldwide recognize. Hearing success stories of their students landing jobs in Singapore and Australia made these certifications feel more tangible and achievable.

Key learnings about career planning:

  • Strategic certifications: Not all certifications are equal - some open more doors
  • Global standards: International certifications make you competitive anywhere
  • Career progression: Understanding entry-level to senior-level certification paths
  • Investment in self: Certifications are expensive but pay for themselves quickly
  • Multiple locations: Having centers in Manila, Cebu, and Davao makes training accessible

As a student, this visit helped me create a roadmap: finish my degree, get CompTIA A+ and Network+, then work toward CCNA. Worldtech made me realize that career success in IT requires both formal education and professional certifications.
